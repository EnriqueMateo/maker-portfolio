# Maker Portfolio — Enrique Mateo Cobo

Mechatronics Engineering student at **KIT (Karlsruher Institut für Technologie)**. I design and build things end to end — mechanics, electronics, firmware and software. I like starting from a blank sheet of graph paper and ending with something that moves, prints, or ships to the App Store.

## How I work

- **From scratch.** Most of these started as a pencil sketch on graph paper, moved into Fusion 360 CAD, and were then printed, wired and tested at my desk.
- **Constraints as a feature.** The printer had a hard budget of €120, so I recycled and reused wherever I could and only bought what I truly needed (linear rails, belts, extrusions, endstops).
- **Iterate on failure.** The first printer frame was fully 3D-printed and flexed too much. I redesigned it around aluminium extrusions with printed reinforcements. Learning by breaking is part of the process.
- **Full stack, both worlds.** I'm as comfortable with kinematics, steppers and G-code as I am with React Native, Node and a production backend.
